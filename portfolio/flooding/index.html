Case Study - Flooding and CSO Prediction Using Flow Sensors


USE CASE: Stormwater


BACKGROUND

Half an inch of rain causes a CSO, but an inch doesn't? In the ticking time bomb of stormwater infrastructure, millions of dollars of critical operational decisions hinge on knowing the answer to one simple question: "Will it flood?" Yet to those on the front lines, even a reliable weather forecast isn't always a good predictor for how your stormwater system will behave. Furthermore, those who know your system best happen to be those who are most nearing retirement. Add to that that many cities and utilities still have no real-time data from their sewer systems, and you can start to see the scope of the problem.

OUTCOME: Know if you will experience a CSO.

By utilizing data from sensors deployed throughout storm and sanitary sewers, Aaron built a model that successfully correlates weather forecasts with CSOs and other overflow events, and provides up to 6 hours of advance notice of flooding at a hyper-local level. Though still a work-in-progress, the model is being deployed in some of the largest and most operationally-important transit hubs in the entire world.
